Design Principles

Stratio's mission is to solve complex problems around the data in the simplest and most intuitive way possible, creating outstanding products that take our users to the next level. To do this, we have a series of essential principles that guide each decision of our design process.

Keep it Simple

Good design emphasises the usefulness of a product: the simpler we make our designs, the faster and easier it is for users to make decisions. Use streamlined interactions and comprehensive UI to bring focus and clarity to user’s core action and information. Making something simple to use is much harder -especially when the underlying systems are complex- but that’s the challenge of Stratio products.

Give Feedback

Implement feedback through notifications, alerts, options and other visual aids, either automatically or according to the user's requests. Provides real-time notification of process status, it makes users feel in control and increase confidence that the task is proceeding and working as intended.

Prioritize Data Visualization

Stratio product is built around data. This must be reflected through interfaces with a focus on data legibility, with visual solutions sparing use of bold and bright colours to get attention on data above a neutral background. Besides, use color coding to convey information quickly, that facilitates visual search.

Be Scalable

Design considering multiple scenarios. Value flexible, scalable solutions that can evolve easily with the content. This principle ensures that the products designed can grow over time.

Design for error prevention

An error is defined as an incorrect user action that prevents you from achieving the desired goal. The system must be prepared to avoid or prevent them. There are many types of errors that can be classified according to their impact.

Focus on details

We care about how our applications look and feel because attractive interfaces have an impact on perceived usability. When the UI has been polished and copywriting is absolutely clear (with context and zero ambiguity), it builds trust with users.
