Design Principles

Universal beliefs validated and scientifically contrasted that affect the human being and his relationship with physical and digital objects, as well as with the world around him. They are based on immutable aspects and when they are present and consolidated, good design is born.

Keep it Simple

Good design emphasises the usefulness of a product: the simpler we make our designs, the faster and easier it is for users to make decisions. Use streamlined interactions and comprehensive UI to bring focus and clarity to user’s core action and information. Making something simple to use is much harder -especially when the underlying systems are complex- but that’s the challenge of Stratio products.

Be Consistent

Use interaction patterns, layout and typography in a consistent way, this helps with usability, and how users perceive the software (efficient and professional). Use whenever possible our framework components and patterns over specific module design element. Maximize productivity and consistency, sharing and reusing instead of reinventing the wheel every time. Use and maintain this cohesive system.

Give Feedback

Implement feedback through notifications, alerts, options and other visual aids, either automatically or according to the user's requests. Provides real-time notification of process status, it makes users feel in control and increase confidence that the task is proceeding and working as intended.

Prioritize Data Visualization

Stratio product is built around data. This must be reflected through interfaces with a focus on data legibility, with visual solutions sparing use of bold and bright colours to get attention on data above a neutral background. Besides, use color coding to convey information quickly, that facilitates visual search.

Be Scalable

Design considering multiple scenarios. Value flexible, scalable solutions that can evolve easily with the content.

Make it Agile

Use design solutions to make our product feel responsive and fast, reflecting our brand. Make it also easy and agile to build and maintain.

Think Modularly

Design components and layouts with an awareness of all the use cases and places they may show up. Be aware of content dimensions and screen sizes.

Focus on details

We are care about how our applications look and feel because attractive interfaces have an impact on perceived usability. When the UI has been polished and copywriting is absolutely clear (with context and zero ambiguity), it builds trust with users.
