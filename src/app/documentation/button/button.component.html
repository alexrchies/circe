Button

Buttons express what action will occur when the user clicks or touches it. Buttons are used to initialize an action, either in the background or foreground of an experience.

Design & Behavior

There are multiple button designs that are related with different scenarios. The label buttons are the usual ones with a solid background and a label or a combination of icon and label.

The tool buttons has not any text, only icons are permitted and are thought on toolbars.

Ths link buttons has the appearance of an HTML link and are thought to secondary actions or scenarios when a more lightweight is needed.

Solid buttons look heavy on purpose. They direct the userâ€™s attention to the primary action the application is suggesting that the user take.

Outline buttons provide a lighter weight button style. They are used to indicate a secondary action that compliments a primary action or to reduce visual noise when there are many action of equal importance on the page.

Flat buttons are used in multiple scenarios. They are used as tertiary buttons. They can also be used inline because they are different from content in style and recognizable as buttons alongside content.
